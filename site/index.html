Nervous System States & Somatic Experience

HYPERAROUSAL — ABOVE WINDOW OF TOLERANCE
SYMPATHETIC NERVOUS SYSTEM — mobilization / fight / flight

All four states below share the same sympathetic substrate (adrenaline + norepinephrine + cortisol sustain). What differs:

valence (threat vs. opportunity) · direction of charge (approach vs. avoidance) · temporal narrative (past / present / future / anticipated good)

Anger / Rage

Neurochemistry

Norepinephrine dominant → approach bias

Adrenaline activation, high charge

Cortisol = sustain if suppressed/chronic

Somatic texture

Heat in face, neck, chest

Chest expands outward, jaw clenches

Hands close, body presses forward

Expanded high-charge (approach)

Rage = brakes offline, same system

Discharge

Physical exertion, yelling (safely)

Vigorous movement, contact sport

Completing a confrontation

Narrative layer

“Something must be stopped”

Present + forward. Action demanded.

Injustice / boundary violation signal.

Valence / Direction

NEGATIVE APPROACH →

Most likely to discharge if action available.

Unresolved → chronic cortisol load.

Suppressed anger → dorsal vagal collapse or somatization over time.

Reappraisal target: justice / agency, not calming down.

↑ sympathetic, expanded, threat-valence

Anxiety

Neurochemistry

Adrenaline + cortisol (sustained)

Simulated threat, no real danger present

Often frozen past fear projected forward

Somatic texture

Chest tight, shallow fast breath

Cold/clammy hands, shoulders raised

Racing thoughts, scanning/vigilance

Contracted high-charge (avoidance)

No clear motor act to discharge into

Discharge

Movement + cognitive close

Long exhale, grounding

Somatic punctuation + reappraisal

Narrative layer

“Something bad is coming” (future)

Forward-facing. Anticipatory threat sim.

May be unresolved present/past fear rerouted.

Valence / Direction

NEGATIVE ← AVOIDANCE

Arousal congruent with excitement — only valence differs (threat vs. opportunity).

Reappraisal to excitement works because arousal level is already the same.

“Calm down” is harder — requires arousal shift (high→low) not just valence shift.

↑ sympathetic, contracted, threat-valence

Fear (Acute)

Neurochemistry

Adrenaline spike — fast onset, fast peak

Cortisol follows if threat persists

Hardest + shortest — needs fast resolution

Somatic texture

Tunnel vision, stomach drops

Muscles lock or mobilize instantly

Breath stops briefly, body pulls inward

Contracted high-charge (exit/freeze)

Hits harder than anxiety, shorter duration

Discharge

Completing the motor act (fight/run)

Shaking, trembling after threat passes

EMDR bilateral stimulation

Narrative layer

“Something bad is here” (present)

Real-time threat computation on NOW.

Most “raw” — least narrative overlay.

Valence / Direction

NEGATIVE ← AVOIDANCE

Most likely to collapse into dorsal vagal if motor act is blocked (inescapable threat).

Trauma = fear response that couldn’t complete. Stored as charge in the body.

Note: excitement ≈ fear but with positive valence (opportunity not threat).

↑ sympathetic, contracted, threat-valence

Excitement

Neurochemistry

Adrenaline + dopamine (anticipation)

Nearly identical profile to anxiety

Dopamine = “approaching reward” signal

Somatic texture

Chest open, buzzing / tingling quality

Fast heart rate, elevated breath

“Butterflies” — same as anxiety

Expands outward (vs. anxiety contracts)

Body leans forward, alert + open

Discharge / Resolution

Outcome occurs → charge completes naturally

Anticipation resolves into experience

But: blocked/cancelled → see paths below ↓

Narrative layer

“Something good is coming” (future)

Forward-facing. Opportunity mindset.

Same temporal slot as anxiety (future).

Valence / Direction

POSITIVE APPROACH →
WHY IT FEELS LIKE ANXIETY

Arousal identical. Appraisal different.

Threat mindset → anxiety. Opportunity → excitement.

Reappraisal works because arousal is already matched.

If blocked / cancelled / postponed

→ Narrative: “something good was taken” — Grief (dorsal collapse) OR anger (re-mobilize)

EXCITEMENT BLOCKED: HOW IT RESOLVES DEPENDS ON THE NARRATIVE

Path A — Grief / Dorsal Collapse

Narrative: “that good thing is now gone / lost” → past-facing

Charge finds no forward motion. System has no motor act to complete.

Sympathetic energy collapses inward → dorsal vagal shutdown, heaviness, numb, flat.

Exit: activate first (sympathetic) → then regulate down to window.

Trigger: “it’s over / it won’t happen / I can’t change it” — helplessness framing.

Path B — Anger / Re-mobilization

Narrative: “that good thing was taken / someone did this” → present + injustice-facing

Charge converts to anger. Sympathetic stays active but shifts to approach/fight mode.

Body presses forward rather than collapsing. Energy has a target.

Discharge: action, confrontation, physical exertion.

Trigger: injustice appraisal. “This was done TO me” → boundary violation signal.

exhale + ground → window
WINDOW OF TOLERANCE — REGULATED
PARASYMPATHETIC — VENTRAL VAGAL BRANCH — safe + social

Somatic texture

Open chest, warmth, breath with depth

Sense of space and capacity

Can think AND feel simultaneously

World reads expansive, not threatening

What the window IS

Not absence of stress — active safety state

Where full function and processing lives

Widens through repeated return from dysreg.

Co-regulation (another person) expands fastest

Entry from hyperarousal

Long exhale (exhale longer than inhale)

Activates vagal tone — not metaphor

Grounding, social signal, slow discharge

Cognitive close + somatic punctuation

Entry from hypoarousal

Must pass through sympathetic first

Cannot go directly — cannot skip this

Activate: slap legs, cold water, loud music

Then regulate DOWN from sympathetic

Ventral vagal = one branch of vagus nerve. Safe ≠ calm. Calm can be shutdown (dorsal vagal). Safety is present + connected + able to respond not react.

This is the only state where somatic/EMDR processing can actually complete. Both excitement resolution and grief processing require returning here first.

Reappraisal (anxiety → excitement) is the fastest cognitive lever to shift valence without changing arousal level — meeting the body where it already is.

Narrative tag: grounded present. No temporal threat story. The raw signal has no timestamp — the story always applies it.

activate first → then regulate down
HYPOAROUSAL — BELOW WINDOW OF TOLERANCE
PARASYMPATHETIC — DORSAL VAGAL BRANCH — shutdown / freeze / collapse

Freeze / Shutdown

Neurochemistry

Vagal brake (acetylcholine) — dumps activation instead of burning it

Opioid system may engage (numbing, dissociation)

Cortisol chronically elevated from prior sustained sympathetic load

Somatic texture

Heavy, numb, foggy, flat affect, collapsed

Dissociated — world feels unreal or distant

Feels like calm but is shutdown — opposite of safe

Pushing on a wall — effort produces nothing

Narrative layer

“Nothing matters / I can’t / it’s over” — helplessness, futility

Grief collapses here when sympathetic charge finds no discharge.

Excitement blocked with helplessness narrative also routes here.

WARNING: trying to “calm down” from here deepens shutdown.

Calming = hyperarousal tool. Activation = hypoarousal tool.

Also parasympathetic — critical: parasympathetic ≠ safe.

Dorsal vagal is older, evolutionary “play dead” branch.

Temporal Narrative Map + Exit Path

Same body signal — narrative is the temporal tag

Anxiety“something bad is coming” (future, negative)
Fear“something bad is here” (present, negative)
Grief“something good is gone” (past, loss)
Excitement“something good is coming” (future, positive)
Anger“something must change now” (present, injustice)

Excitement blocked → grief (collapse) OR anger (re-mobilize) depending on narrative.

Exit path from hypoarousal (cannot skip)

Step 1 — Activate: slap legs, cold water, loud music, jumping

Step 2 — Land in sympathetic (on purpose — this is correct)

Step 3 — Regulate down: long exhale, grounding, slow breath

Step 4 — Arrive at ventral vagal window

Skipping steps 1+2 = pushing on the wall. Will not move.

EMDR note

Works on the body-level charge before the narrative. The raw signal was never temporally tagged — only the story applies the timestamp.
